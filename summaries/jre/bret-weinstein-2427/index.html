What's New

Bret Weinstein has been deeply engaged in proposing new hypotheses in evolutionary biology, particularly emphasizing mechanisms beyond random mutations. His concept of "hypernovelty" addresses how the rapid pace of technological and societal change is outstripping human adaptability—a theme he explores extensively in his ongoing work with Heather on their book A Hunter-Gatherer's Guide to the 21st Century.

His public presence has significantly increased, with appearances on numerous podcasts discussing everything from evolution to public health. The COVID-19 pandemic became a crucible for his thinking, leading him to engage with figures like Pierre Kory on medical freedom and alternative treatments. He's collaborated with Charlie Kirk on challenging vaccine availability policies for children and pregnant women—work Kirk described as "holy work together."

Weinstein has become increasingly vocal about what he sees as institutional failures during the pandemic, critiquing the suppression of alternative treatments and questioning the motivations behind pharmaceutical industry actions. He's also raised alarms about emerging threats to free speech, central bank digital currencies, and the broader erosion of constitutional protections—concerns that have shifted his focus toward cultural and political commentary alongside his scientific work.

Evolved Thinking

Weinstein's understanding of evolution has undergone significant refinement. Where he once viewed evolution primarily through the lens of random mutations and gradual changes, he now sees it as a far more intricate mechanism involving stored variables in DNA that allow for rapid adaptation—a perspective informed by developmental biology and his concept of "explorer modes."

On stability versus change: He now recognizes that some species like crocodiles and sharks have thrived by remaining largely unchanged for millions of years, suggesting that stability can be just as successful an evolutionary strategy as constant adaptation.

His views on public health have shifted dramatically. He initially supported masks and vaccination during COVID-19 but has since admitted error regarding mask efficacy, acknowledging a lack of evidentiary support. He now expresses skepticism about vaccine necessity for healthy individuals, particularly those who have recovered from the illness—a significant departure from his earlier positions.

On public health and tyranny: What he once viewed as protective measures he now sees as potential tools of control, particularly regarding central bank digital currencies and restrictions on free speech. "A society needs to be able to talk about this, and this is happening at exactly the moment when the society in question is losing the ability to talk freely."

His perspective on modern relationships and technology has darkened. He now expresses genuine concern that AI companions and sex robots could fundamentally alter human connection, diminishing motivation for personal growth and meaningful relationships. Where he once held more traditional views on social structures, he now critiques how the sexual revolution and technology have created confusion and anxiety among young people.

Perhaps most notably, Weinstein has embraced the importance of acknowledging errors publicly. He argues that admitting mistakes is crucial for intellectual integrity—a maturation in his approach to public discourse that reflects hard-won wisdom from the pandemic years.

One to two minutes for stories told

Stories Told

The Accidental Ivermectin Experiment

Weinstein recounts a striking case where families sued hospitals for refusing to administer ivermectin to critically ill COVID patients. In cases where courts ordered the treatment, 38 out of 40 patients survived—compared to only 2 out of 40 who didn't receive it. He presents this as an accidental natural experiment that was largely ignored by the medical establishment.

Medical freedom COVID treatment Institutional failure

The Phone Call with Sam Harris

A recent phone conversation with Sam Harris became a point of genuine bewilderment for Weinstein. Despite his healthy lifestyle and successful recovery from COVID-19, Harris insisted he should still get vaccinated. Weinstein describes the exchange as exemplifying the "complete illogical thinking" that pervaded pandemic discourse.

Intellectual debate Natural immunity Friendship tested

Holy Work with Charlie Kirk

Weinstein shares a personal moment from his collaboration with Charlie Kirk on challenging COVID-19 vaccine policies for children and pregnant women. Kirk texted him: "We're doing holy work together." The phrase resonated deeply with Weinstein, illustrating the unexpected alliances formed during the contentious public health period.

Unlikely alliance Advocacy Personal connection

The Reproductive Health Crisis

Drawing on research from Dr. Shanna Swan, Weinstein describes alarming findings about declining male reproductive health and rising miscarriage rates—trends he attributes to environmental factors like microplastics. He frames this as evidence that modern environmental changes are threatening the very foundations of human biology.

Environmental health Fertility crisis Modern toxins

The COVID Test Nobody Passed

Reflecting on institutional responses during the pandemic, Weinstein observes that almost no organization—including religious communities—passed what he calls "the COVID test." The widespread failure to maintain principles under pressure revealed deep vulnerabilities in institutions people had trusted.

Institutional failure Social trust Pandemic lessons
