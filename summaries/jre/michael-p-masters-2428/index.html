Origin Story

Michael P. Masters' fascination with UFOs and extraterrestrial life began in childhood, around the age of eight or nine, when he experienced a vivid moment of imagination connecting early hominins, modern humans, and the archetypal gray aliens. This early intrigue planted the seeds for what would become a lifelong exploration of humanity's place in the universe.

A defining moment came through a story told by his father, a veterinarian in Ohio, about witnessing a bright light in the sky that moved in an extraordinary manner—an experience that profoundly influenced Masters' interest in UFOs and the concept of time travel.

Masters pursued a degree in anthropology, specializing in evolutionary anatomy and biomedicine. Initially, his academic work was distinct from his interest in UFOs, but he later integrated these fields in a groundbreaking way—proposing that UFOs and aliens could represent future human descendants traveling back in time. His academic training equipped him to explore these unconventional ideas through a rigorous scientific lens.

A significant crossroads occurred when he decided to publicly share his theories about UFOs, risking his academic credibility. Encouragement from peers and mentors, including a supportive department chair, helped him navigate this decision. The experience reinforced his belief in the importance of asking challenging questions and engaging with controversial subjects, even when facing potential professional consequences.

Masters believes that investigating UFO phenomena can shed light on fundamental existential questions about consciousness and humanity's role in the universe—bridging scientific inquiry with the often-marginalized field of UFO studies.

Throughout his career, Masters has collaborated with researchers from prestigious institutions, including Harvard. His work has gained recognition through opportunities to discuss his theories in public forums, such as podcasts and conferences. His willingness to engage with controversial topics—including genetic manipulation and breakaway civilizations—marks a significant evolution in his approach.

In 2022, Masters shared a personal experience described as a "mini-abduction" during a UFO conference, where he encountered a stranger who seemed to connect with him telepathically. This experience reinforced his belief in the possibility of future humans or advanced beings interacting with humanity, deepening his commitment to exploring these themes. He has also developed a course on UFOs at Montana Tech, reflecting the growing acceptance of these topics within the academic community.

Stories Told

Betty and Barney Hill Abduction

The first widely publicized alien abduction case in the United States, which took place in 1961. Betty and Barney Hill, an interracial couple, reported being abducted by extraterrestrial beings while driving home. They experienced lost time and vivid dreams of their encounter, with elements that resonate with various religious narratives—highlighting the societal stigma surrounding discussions of such experiences.

Abduction Stigma Historical

Personal UFO Encounter in Montana

Masters shares a personal experience of witnessing five bright lights in the sky while living in Montana. The lights exhibited movements suggesting intelligent control, defying conventional explanations. This encounter challenged his preconceived notions of reality and reinforced the importance of open-mindedness when exploring the unknown.

Personal experience Montana Unexplained

Time Travel and Future Humans Theory

Masters discusses his theory that UFOs and aliens may be time-traveling future humans. As humanity evolves, it may develop advanced technologies allowing for time travel and the need to revisit the past for genetic material. This narrative encourages a re-evaluation of what it means to be human and the potential consequences of technological advancements.

Time travel Evolution Genetics

The Baltic Sea Anomaly

The discussion explores a sonar-detected seafloor formation found in 2011 by a treasure-hunting team. The formation has sparked speculation about its origins, with some suggesting it could be an ancient structure rather than a natural formation—raising questions about human history and the potential for undiscovered ancient civilizations.

Baltic Sea Ancient structures Mystery

Zulu Lore and the Sky Goddess

Masters recounts a Zulu legend about a sky goddess who chooses a man to mate with, communicating telepathically and taking him away on a rainbow of light. This ancient story parallels modern accounts of alien abduction, suggesting that cultural narratives across time and geography may reflect deeper experiences with the unknown.

Zulu folklore Telepathy Parallels
