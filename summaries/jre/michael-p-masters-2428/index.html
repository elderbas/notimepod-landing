Origin Story

Michael P. Masters' journey into the intersection of anthropology and UFO research began with a childhood fascination that most would dismiss as mere imagination. Around the age of eight or nine, he experienced a vivid moment connecting early hominins, modern humans, and the archetypal gray aliens—an image that would plant the seeds for his life's work.

A defining moment came from his father, a veterinarian in Ohio, who witnessed a bright light in the sky that moved in extraordinary ways. This story profoundly influenced Masters' interest in UFOs and the concept of time travel.

Masters pursued a traditional academic path, earning degrees in anthropology with specializations in evolutionary anatomy and biomedicine. Initially, his scholarly work remained distinct from his interest in UFOs, but he eventually found a way to integrate these seemingly disparate fields. His breakthrough theory proposes that UFOs and aliens could represent future human descendants traveling back in time—a hypothesis he calls the "extratempestrial" model.

A significant crossroads occurred when he decided to publicly share his theories, risking his academic credibility. Encouragement from peers and mentors, including a supportive department chair, helped him navigate this decision. The diminishing stigma surrounding UFO discussions in recent years has allowed him to engage more openly with these controversial subjects.

"Once you know who we are, you'll know how we know that"—a statement that encapsulates his belief that understanding human evolution may unlock the mysteries of UFO phenomena.

His academic work has evolved to include a course on UFOs at Montana Tech, reflecting a growing acceptance within academia. Personal experiences have reinforced his commitment, including what he describes as a "mini-abduction" during a 2022 UFO conference, where he encountered a stranger who seemed to connect with him telepathically.

Throughout his career, Masters has collaborated with researchers from prestigious institutions, including Harvard. His books—Identified Flying Objects, The Extratempestrial Model, and Revelation: The Future Human Past—explore the connections between anthropology, consciousness, and the UFO phenomenon, challenging readers to reconsider humanity's place in the cosmos.

Stories Told

The Betty and Barney Hill Abduction

Masters recounts the first widely publicized alien abduction case in American history. In 1961, Betty and Barney Hill, an interracial couple, reported being abducted by extraterrestrial beings while driving home. They experienced lost time and vivid dreams of their encounter, which included elements that resonate with various religious narratives. The case highlights the societal stigma surrounding discussions of traumatic experiences involving UFOs.

Alien abduction Historical UFO cases Stigma

Five Lights Over Montana

Masters shares a personal experience of witnessing five bright lights in the sky while living in Montana. The lights exhibited movements suggesting intelligent control, defying conventional explanations for natural or man-made phenomena. This encounter reinforced his belief that such experiences, while difficult to prove, form part of a larger pattern worthy of scientific investigation.

Personal encounter UFO sighting Intelligent movement

Time-Traveling Future Humans

The central theory Masters proposes: UFOs and their occupants may be time-traveling future humans returning to the past for genetic material. As humanity evolves and potentially faces issues with genetic diversity and reproduction, our descendants might develop technologies allowing temporal travel. This framework connects evolutionary biology with UFO phenomena in a scientifically grounded hypothesis.

Time travel Human evolution Genetics

The Baltic Sea Anomaly

Masters discusses a sonar-detected seafloor formation discovered in 2011 by a treasure-hunting team in the Baltic Sea. The formation has sparked speculation about its origins, with some suggesting it could be an ancient structure rather than a natural geological feature. The anomaly serves as a reminder of how much remains unknown about our planet's history and the possibility of undiscovered ancient civilizations.

Underwater anomaly Ancient civilizations Mystery

The Zulu Sky Goddess

Masters recounts a Zulu legend about a sky goddess who chooses a man to mate with, communicating telepathically and taking him away on a rainbow of light. This ancient narrative parallels modern accounts of alien abduction, suggesting that similar experiences have been reported throughout human history, long before our modern conception of UFOs emerged.

Ancient lore Cultural parallels Telepathy
